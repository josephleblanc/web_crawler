Prologue: The Two Souls

The boy with two souls awoke to the cries of gulls. 

He opened his eyes and stared dumbly at the sky. Tiny white specks circled and dove amidst the backdrop of the burning sun. The smell of salt rang in the air. Sand shifted beneath him when he moved. 

“Where…am I?” he mumbled. 

I need to stand up and survey the surroundings, a deep, gruff voice said in his ear. Establish a defensive… wait. What is this?

“Gah!” the boy shouted, scrambling to his feet. He looked around wildly and only saw an indigo sea hemmed in by sand. Seafoam gurgled on the ebbing waves. But nobody else was here…

His arm suddenly jerked to the left, and he yelped. 

Aha! the voice said triumphantly. Now I’m getting the hang of it!

“Hey!” the boy snapped at his left arm flailing about randomly. “Quit it!”

What kind of dark magic have you done to me, boy?!

His traitorous left hand reached up and grabbed a fistful of his hair, shaking him about. 

“Stop that!” the boy yelled, gripping his left arm with his right. “Hey, give me my arm back!” 

[SYSTEM ERROR]: THIS BEING EXCEEDS THE MAXIMUM AMOUNT OF SOULS ALLOTTED FOR ITS RACE. TERMINATION IN 5… 4…

“Termination?!” the boy said. 

Termination?! the man in his head said.

[SYSTEM MESSAGE]: TERMINATION CANCELLED. SPECIAL PERMIT DESIGNATED. UNIQUE SYSTEM INITIALIZING…

Hey, kid, the voice said. You read those messages?

“Don’t ‘Hey, kid,” me as if everything’s normal! Get out of my head!” 

Settle down. Didn’t you read the messages? We got some special kind of permit. 

“And? A simple message is supposed to make me okay with some stranger speaking directly into my head?” 

Not the brightest, are we, boy? I’m not speaking directly into your head, I’m IN your head. IN your body. With deduction skills like that, you’d be eaten alive on the battlefield. 
 
The boy gritted his teeth. Despite the truth in the invaders words, he couldn’t help but feel disarray. There’s another being inside of him! Was he supposed to just accept that and move on? 

He sighed. 

“H-how are you so okay with this? This is insane! I have no idea where I am, how I got here, and I’m supposed to now walk around with another person IN my body?” he said. 

There’s no point in worrying about what you can’t control, kid. You need to learn to accept things the way they are and make do with what you’ve been given. Instead of wasting energy worrying, put it to use on more productive things, like finding out where the hell we are. Now get to your feet. We need to get a grasp on what in Kravan’s name is going on here. 

The boy frowned and opened his mouth to retort but shut it soon after. There was a air of authority and confidence in the intruders voice, and the boy couldn’t help but feel compelled by it. Not that he was happy about it…

“Ok, ok. I’ll check our surroundings. I doubt we’ll find anything but sand though…” 

*Ding!* 

A loud, unnatural sound rang through the air before the boy could get to his feet. 

Dual System Initialized.

Greater | Lvl. 10  - Ironshell Crab

Chapter One: Ironshell Crab

Arthur did a weird half-dash forward before lurching to a halt, his bare feet skidding in the sand.

“Hey!” he snapped at Iskander, who he could feel urging to run forward. “Knock it off! How do you think I’m gonna beat a crab like this?”

Worry not, Arthur. You fear it because you lack experience, not because it's insurmountable. With my guidance, we’ll clear this tu…torial? Right? Whatever it is! We’ll clear it and make off with our rewards!

Arthur’s disagreement was almost audible as he took shaking steps back.

“No way. That’s not happening. It's the size of an elephant! I don’t know what battles you’ve fought in your life, but no ordinary human can beat that stampeding, elephant-sized crab. We’re running!”

But again, his body wasn’t fully listening to his commands. He had gone to turn tail and escape, but his legs were like stone, dragging heavily through the sand.

“Iskander! You need to stop this or you’re gonna get us both killed!” he shouted, his heart beating in his throat.

Hmm? I didn’t do anything, runt! I only thought it would be nice if you could work with me here so we can do what we’ve been tasked with doing. Perhaps my will is overpowering that of your own; a power struggle, if you will, Iskander said with more gloat than Arthur liked.

So what are you saying? Arthur complained to himself. That I’m spineless?

The crab lumbered forward, each of its pincers large enough to cut Arthur in half. Long strands of seaweed clung to its shell like tattered clothes. Its beady eyes were utterly empty.

Arthur gripped his fists till his knuckles turned white. “Tell me, Iskander. If we fight…can we win?”

I don’t know. There’s no battle in this world that's won before you fight it. But if we let uncertainty hold us back, we’ll never be able to move forward. Throw caution to the wind and trust in me, I won’t let you die!

The resistance in Arthur’s legs vanished. If he wanted he was free to run for the hills and never see that crab ever again. But if such desires still drove him, perhaps his legs would still be shaking?

Arthur dashed towards the crab, his breathing ragged with fear.

The walking boulder picked up its pace with each step it took, slowly coming into a charge.

“What’s the plan?” Arthur shouted.

Now that’s more like it! Iskander said. We need to use its claws against it. The tutorial thing said it, but I also can’t see how we’re expected to defeat it any other way.

“I get your line of thought, but how will that help me kill it? You do have a plan, right!?”

Our skills! Soul Push! Soul Pull! Did you not read what they do?

“What? You did? How in the world did you do that?”

Iskander laughed. The bloody bastard laughed at him!

I should’ve expected as much from you, kid. I’ll explain that after we win. For now, I’m going to need you to use Push on its claws. Get them as high in the air as you possibly can, understand? We have no time to hesitate, I’ll guide you as you go!

Use Push? Was Arthur supposed to just instinctively understand how to do that? He thought back to the shiny blue screen that appeared just moments ago, focusing his memory on the words “Soul Push” labeled under his Skills.

The crab was closer now, its claws open and clamping like a vice ready to snap Arthur’s head off his body.

Use Push on its claws. Use Push on its claws, Arthur thought over and over to himself.

Closer. Closer. The crab was right upon them.

Use the Skill, kid! Iksander commanded.

“[Soul Push]!” Arthur shouted.

In the base of his feet, Arthur felt a power surge. It flowed through his legs and into his torso like a river before coursing into his outstretched hand.

Perfect! Now slide underneath it and swap with m—

[Soul Push Failed | Target Outside of Applicability]

“Eh?”

The crab gurgled, as if in defiance to Arthur’s Push, and swung its claws towards him.

Get out of the way, Arthur! Iskander shouted.

Arthur was still. His eyes locked on the pair of claws that barrelled towards him.

An image flashed in his mind. Lights. Blindingly bright lights. Glass, sharp as razor blades. Bones breaking…

Suddenly, Arthur felt his legs move on their own, throwing him to the ground and into a tumble. The claws snapped high over the crab’s head with a resounding crack.

Come… on… kid… Iskander grunted, and Arthur’s body awkwardly rolled out of the way as the crab made another attack. He sucked in a shaky breath and scrambled to his feet and ran.

“I’m…I’m sorry,” Arthur mumbled.

Don’t be sorry! Just focus on not making the same mistake. I’m partly to blame for this seeing as my plan was flawed. We’re too weak to enact our skills upon it.

The fuzz in Arthur’s mind finally cleared. “Then…we can’t fight it? What are we supposed to do, then!”

Iskander willed Arthur backwards as the crabs claw swiped past his face, slicing a thin cut on the round of his cheek.

Pay attention! You don’t get to lose focus on the battlefield!

Arthur grit his teeth. “Stop taking control of my body! If you would've just let me handle things, we wouldn’t be in this mess anyways!”

Then you need to focus! If you let your will slip, mine will come through. So you need to keep your wits about you and—

Again, Arthur had his body hijacked as Iskander pushed him out of the way of yet another attack.

—and stop dozing off in the middle of battle! Iskander snapped as Arthur spat sand out of his mouth. He shoved himself to his feet and darted out of the crab’s path.

“I would’ve moved out of the way myself,” Arthur growled under his breath. “Leave my body alone.”

Iskander scoffed. A little gratitude is in order, kid.

The crab charged forward, big bunches of sand thrown to the side in its wake. Arthur tried to run to the right on instinct, but Iskander tried to push his legs to go to the left, away from the sea.

“Iskander, stop!”

Kid, just listen to me!

The crab barrelled forward, pincers out.

Arthur gasped and stumbled in place, the two motions cancelling each other out. Iskander grunted and went silent. A wave of dizzying vertigo flooded through Arthur, and he dropped to his knees, dazed. His vision blurred, and in one eye he saw the crab and the palm trees and the blue sky; but in the other he was on a road as a truck thundered toward him, and screams reached towards the stars…

Someone, save me! Arthur screamed inwardly.

From the distance, a projectile. It cut through the air like a hot knife through soft cheese and slammed between him and the crab with a resounding impact, sending sand spraying everywhere.

A spear? Iskander thought in a slightly shaken tone.

A high-pitched whine rang in Arthur’s ears before resolving into a deep hum, and with a flash of blue light a shirtless man appeared beside the spear. Deeply tanned with long brown hair, he only wore a pair of linen shorts and cork sandals, and in his left hand was a bag of still-squirming fish.

With his right hand, he gripped the spear and made a smooth swinging motion. The tiny bells that hung off the tassel at the end of the hilt rang lightly in the air as a powerful gust of wind picked up and blew against the crab.

The crab skidded to a halt. It clacked its pincers nervously.

“Go,” the man ordered, pointing a stern finger at the crustacean. “Or I’ll eat you.”

The crab gurgled and moved forward a bit before deciding against it. With an angry clack of its claws, it quickly scurried back to the sea.

Shaken and confused, Arthur let loose a tightly coiled breath. “Oh thank God—”

[SYSTEM MESSAGE]: CHEATING DETECTED. OUTSIDE ENTITIES CAN NOT INTERFERE WITH A TUTORIAL QUEST. TUTORIAL QUEST PRIVILEGES REVOKED.

Wait, what? Iskander said.

“Our tutorial quest privileges are revoked?” Arthur read out loud in disbelief.

“Tutorial quest?” the man echoed, shaking the sand off his spear. “No, no, that's impossible. You cannot be here on Lagros if you have only just engaged with the System. Wait, did you just say….‘our’?”

[SYSTEM MESSAGE]: FURTHERMORE, ANY ADDITIONAL HELP BY THE SYSTEM WILL NO LONGER BE GIVEN.

“You’ve got to be kidding me….” Arthur muttered.
